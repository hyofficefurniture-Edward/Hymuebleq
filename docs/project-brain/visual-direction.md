# Visual Direction

- Last updated: 2026-06-29
- Objective: move Hymueble from a competent B2B prototype toward a premium international project-furniture brand experience.

## Visual Keywords

- Architectural
- Spatial
- Editorial
- Premium manufacturing
- Project confidence
- Controlled contrast
- Warm metallic CTA
- Immersive showroom
- Calm procurement clarity

## Reference Study Notes

External references are used only for direction, not for copying assets or layouts.

- Georgebuild is now the primary layout reference for the v0.59 direction: spacious B2B building-materials composition, large editorial headings, light hero surface, oversized right-side scene image, dark showroom/experience sections, image-led product/category cards, restrained gold CTA, and strong footer/proof rhythm. The implementation should borrow layout language, not copy assets, text, or a pixel-identical page.
- Awwwards furniture references show that premium furniture sites often rely on clean animation, parallax, transitions, gallery behavior, and controlled palettes rather than dense product grids.
- Charter Furniture positions hospitality furniture through luxury hotel spaces, collaboration with design firms, and custom manufacturing, which supports Hymueble's project-supply narrative.
- Home'Society and Norwalk virtual showroom references show that showroom copy should explain remote exploration, area-based browsing, and the feeling of being inside a real showroom.
- Furniture UX references emphasize scale, context photography, 3D/virtual exploration, configurators, and detailed product decision support.

## Color Direction

- Base background: `#F8F7F4`
- Main text: `#121D29`
- Headline: `#121A22`
- CTA gold: `#C2A378`
- Dark brand field: near-black blue `#0F171F`
- Supporting green: restrained trust tone, not dominant

The site should not become a one-hue beige or dark-blue theme. Gold is a CTA and highlight color, not a full-page wash.

## Typography Direction

- Large editorial H1 for hero only.
- Compact, sharp section headings.
- Dense but readable B2B copy.
- No negative letter spacing.
- Keep button labels short and high-contrast.

## Homepage Hero Direction

The home hero should become an immersive spatial scene:

- Full-bleed or near full-bleed project-space image.
- Dark overlay or controlled cinematic crop.
- Large headline over the spatial image.
- Immediate proof chips: factory direct, sectors, export, quality control.
- Strong CTA pair: quotation and Virtual Showroom.
- A visible showroom experience card rather than a small ordinary link.

## Image Style

- Prefer spaces over isolated products.
- Prefer hotel lobby, office, showroom, factory, and project context.
- Use product cards only when they point to project selection.
- Avoid watermarked, low-resolution, unrelated, or overly generic stock-like images.
- Do not repeat the same image across many modules just to fill the layout. If a section needs a scene image and no suitable local asset exists, generate high-quality furniture/interior scenes that avoid visible people. If people are necessary for scale, prefer backs, silhouettes, partial figures, or non-identifying distant staff.

## Motion Principles

- Use stable CSS transitions and transform-based hover.
- Subtle image scale, card lift, overlay reveal, and showroom pulse are acceptable.
- Avoid heavy motion that hurts readability or page stability.

## Card System

- Cards stay at 8px radius or less.
- Cards are for repeated items and tools, not nested page sections.
- Premium cards should use sharp spacing, image dominance, concise metadata, and clear action.

## Virtual Showroom Strategy

Virtual Showroom should be treated as a conversion asset:

- Appears in the homepage hero.
- Has its own immersive section.
- Uses dark background, spatial image, glow/outline accent, and CTA.
- Links to Matterport when available: `https://my.matterport.com/show/?m=XJAMZN562aG`.
- Also routes back to contact for scheduled support.

## Mobile Principles

- Keep H1 powerful but not cramped.
- Stack CTA buttons cleanly.
- Do not hide the showroom path on mobile.
- Avoid horizontal overflow.
- Keep tap targets large and text readable.

## Not Allowed

- Plain factory-template hero.
- Product-grid-only homepage.
- Decorative blobs or random gradients.
- Text-heavy first screen with weak imagery.
- Showroom entry as a small secondary button only.
